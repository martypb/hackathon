What is a hackathon?

A hackathon (also known as a hack day, hackfest or codefest) is an event in which computer programmers and others involved in software development, including graphic designers, interface designers and project managers, collaborate intensively on software projects.

The goal?

Make a working prototype in 24 hours without the usual overheads. You choose the stack. You choose the hosting. Don't like the work PC? BYOD!

Who can participate?

All of CommSec is invited - To build a great solution in just 24 hours we need great collaboration between people of many different skills.

  • Can you code or help to make great software?
  • Can you spot commercial opportunities?
  • Do you have an eye for detail and design?
  • Do you give an amazing elevator pitch?
  • Do you have an idea of how to make CommSec even better?
If you answered "Yes" to any of those questions, we want you there!

Event Structure

Subject to change, but this will be the rough plan for the event on Sept 19th-20th:
  • Registration - people who are participating sign-in or register at the terrace on level 6
  • Idea pitches, teams forming - participants with ideas get a short time to pitch their idea to everyone. Small teams get formed by those with ideas in common
  • Code window - 24 hrs in which to make some awesome solutions
  • Pitch to the judges - You'll get to demonstrate what you've done to the judges
  • Pitch to the crowd - The judges select some competitors to proceed through to pitch to everyone present
  • Prizes and celebration - Winners are chosen, prizes handed out, then we all go and celebrate

The Rules

A few things general guidelines to follow if you decide to participate.
  • Rule 1: Don't be an jerk.
  • Rule 2: All new code must be completed before cut-off
  • Rule 3: Judge's decisions are final.
Also note at this point that whatever Intellectual Property is created during the event will be owned by CommSec.

FAQs

Q. Will there be food & drinks provided?

A. Dinner, breakfast and lunch will be provided, along with energy drinks and snacks to keep you going. Rumours of free beer cannot be confirmed or denied at this time.

Q. Do we have to stay on site for 24 hours?

A. No. The competition runs for 24hrs but you can come and go as you please.

Q. Can I prepare images/copy/design/storyboards/ideas beforehand? Or do i have to do that during the competition?

A. You can prepare all images, logos, text, ideas beforehand. You can even rehearse your pitch and research your market if you like. The one thing you cannot do is prepare any code.

Q. Can I use existing software that is available on the web?

A. Yes, but it's recommended that you are up front about acrediting it to the judges - credit where credit is due!

Q. How will I juggle this with my regular responsibilties

A. People participating in the hackathon should discuss this with their team and their manager at the earliest opportunity to ensure their unavailability isn't going to cause problems.
